  • Bent-Over Row

    - Primary Muscles: Latissimus Dorsi, Rhomboids
    - Secondary muscles: Trapezius
    - Equipment: Dumbbells, Performance Barbell Bench Press, Precor DB Bench

    Bent-Over Row Instructions

    - Grab a set of dumbbells, bend your torso forward and keep your knees slightly bent.
    - Pull the dumbbells toward your waistline, while squeezing your shoulder blades.
    - Slowly lower the weights to the starting position. A 1-second pull, 1-second pause, 2 second down count is ideal.
    - Repeat.

    Exercise Benefits

    Adding the dumbbell bent over row to your workout routine helps to sculpt your back, shoulders, biceps, and forearms.
    This exercise also strengthens your core and helps to tone and tighten your entire body.

  • Standing cable row

    - Primary Muscle Groups: Biceps, Lats (back)
    - Secondary Muscle Groups: Rear Shoulders, Upper Abs
    - Equipment: Strength Machine

    Standing cable row Instructions

    - Do not lean forward.
    - Keep your back flat and core tight.
    - Keep your chest up and pinch your shoulder blades together to avoid letting your shoulders roll forward.

    Exercise Benefits

    The cable row standing targets the upper back and shoulders while increasing strength and stability in the lower body and core.

  • Bent over row

    - Primary muscles: Back
    - Secondary muscles: Abs, shoulders, arms
    - Equipment: Dumbbells

    Bent over row Instructions

    - Grab a set of dumbbells, bend your torso forward and keep your knees slightly bent.
    - Pull the dumbbells toward your waistline, while squeezing your shoulder blades.
    - Slowly lower the weights to the starting position. A 1-second pull, 1-second pause, 2 second down count is ideal.
    - Repeat.

    Exercise Benefits

    The bent over row is a multi-jointed exercise that recruits several different muscles. It improves strength in the upper and lower back, glutes, hamstrings, lats, and shoulders.
    This makes it an awesome tool for targeting the muscles and portions of the lift that help with breaking through plateaus for improving strength for pull-ups, muscle-ups and even pushing exercises like bench press, push-ups or ring dips!

  • Inverted row TRX

    - Primary Muscle Groups: Lats (back), Lower Back, Rhomboids
    - Secondary Muscle Groups: Biceps, Upper Abs
    - Equipment: Rings

    Inverted row TRX Instructions

    - Hang the TRX cables on something high.
    - Grab the handles and hang down directly under the ropes.
    - Let your arms extend up.
    - With your palms facing inwards pull your body up to the handles just below your chest.
    - Lower yourself back down.
    - This completes one repetition.

    Exercise Benefits

    Inverted rows are an excellent addition to a full-body workout.
    Overall, they can improve upper body strength and grip strength, recruit the glutes and hamstrings, and give your biceps a boost.
