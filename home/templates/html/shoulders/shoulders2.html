  • deltoid raise machine

    - Primary muscle : the medial or middle deltoid. Secondary muscles : Equipment:
    - Secondary muscles : none
    - Equipment: dumbbells/barbell/machine.

    deltoid raise machine Instructions

    1. Begin by selecting the weight you wish to use on the stack of a lateral raise machine.
    2. Adjust the seat height and sit facing the machine with your feet flat on the floor around shoulder width apart.
    3. Secure your arms in the padding and grip the handles. Look straight ahead. You are now ready to begin the exercise.
    4. With a bend in the elbows and moving only at the shoulders, begin pushing the weight up until your forearms are just above parallel.
    5. Contract your shoulders at the height of the movement and begin slowly lowering the weight using the same semicircle motion you used raise it.
    6. Repeat this movement for desired reps.

    Exercise Benefits

    Though commonly viewed as a physique-focused exercise, the benefits of the lateral raise extend beyond that of muscle hypertrophy. When performed well, the lateral raise also improves shoulder health, reinforces proper bracing and stability, and teaches the user to isolate individual muscle groups..

  • One Arm Dumbbell Lateral Raise

    - Primary muscle : the medial or middle deltoid
    - Secondary muscles: anterior deltoid, posterior deltoid, serratus anterior
    - Equipment: dumbbells/barbell/machine

    One Arm Dumbbell Lateral Raise Instructions

    1. Grab two dumbbells and stand up straight with your feet and knees together holding the dumbbells down at your sides with palms facing in.
    2. Hold the dumbbells out at around 4 inches from your body. This is the starting position for the exercise.
    3. Keeping your body still, eyes facing forward, and arms straight, slowly raise the left dumbbell up to around shoulder height.
    4. Pause, and then slowly lower the dumbbell back to starting position.
    5. Repeat for desired reps and, and then repeat with your right arm

    Exercise Benefits

    • Builds shoulder strength and size, even with light weights.
    • Can help create balanced shoulder development between sides.
    • Easy to increase range of motion by holding on to a post and leaning to the side.
    • Can create better mind-muscle connection than raises using two arms.

  • standing lateral raise

    - Primary muscle : the medial or middle deltoid
    - Secondary muscles : anterior deltoid
    Equipment: dumbbells/barbell/machine

    standing lateral raise Instructions

    1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and slightly bend your hips and knees. Hold dumbbells at your sides with your palms facing in and bend your elbows slightly.
    2. Tighten your core and raise the dumbbells to the side until your upper arms are no higher than shoulder height.
    3. Lower the dumbbells with control to return to the starting position.

    Exercise Benefits

    Performing the Lateral Raise builds larger deltoids and help you achieve the boulder shoulder look that many people desire. Even though it’s typically performed with a light weight, you will feel a brutal burn in your shoulders and a subsequent muscle pump that results in growth. Theoretically, having larger deltoids should help to prevent upper-body injuries by improving the muscles’ ability to absorb contact from a tackle, bodycheck or simply falling to the ground.
