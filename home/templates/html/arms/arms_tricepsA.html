  • Close Grip Bench Press

    - Primary muscle : Triceps
    - Secondary muscles : chest, and shoulders
    - Equipment: Barbell and bench

    Close Grip Bench Press Instructions

    1. A spotter or smith machine is recommended for safety.
    2. Use a flat bench station or flat bench/power rack combination
    3. Position the barbell at the correct reach level on the rack (you are able to grip/lift the bar off the rack with assistance).
    4. Load weight resistance according to your fitness level onto the barbell.
    5. Lie flat on the bench using a close grip (about shoulder width).
    6. Lift the bar with assistance from the rack, arms locked, and holding bar straight over you.
    7. Inhale and slowly bring the bar down toward your chest keeping elbows close to your body for the entire exercise.
    8. Exhale and push the bar up using the triceps muscles and locking arms at the top of the movement.
    9. Repeat the exercise for the recommended number of repetitions.
    10. Return the bar to the rack upon exercise completion.

    Exercise Benefits

    • Builds Strong triceps.
    • Improves Bench Press Lockout.
    • Reduce Elbow Flaring.
    • More Upper Chest Activation.
    • Carries Over to Sports.
    • Better for Lifters with Long Arms.
    • Time Efficient and Convenient.
    • Powerlifters and Strongmen.

  • Triceps Dips

    - Primary muscle : Triceps
    - Secondary muscles : chest
    - Equipment: Chair or bench

    Triceps Dips Instructions

    1. Press into your palms to lift your body and slide forward just far enough that your behind clears the edge of the chair.
    2. Lower yourself until your elbows are bent between 45 and 90 degrees. Control the movement throughout the range of motion.
    3. Push yourself back up slowly until your arms are almost straight and repeat.

    Exercise Benefits

    The triceps dip is one of the most effective exercises for activating the triceps brachii muscle in the back of your upper arm.1 Additionally, you must activate your core as you hold your hips off the ground. We use our triceps for extending our elbows.2 They're also used for pushing, so you engage them in any daily activities that require a pushing motion. This includes pushing a lawnmower or a shopping cart and other similar movements. It's also important to keep your body in balance. So, if you participate in sports that use a lot of pulling actions (which involve the biceps), you need to maintain strength in your triceps in order to prevent muscle imbalances and injury.

  • Seated Dumbbell Overhead Triceps Extension

    - Primary muscle : Triceps
    - Secondary muscles : None
    - Equipment: Dumbbells

    Seated Dumbbell Overhead Triceps Extension Instructions

    1. Grab a dumbbell and sit tall on a bench. Brace your core—as if you’re about to get punched in the gut—and squeeze your glutes. Maintain these contractions the entire exercise.
    2. Hold one end of the weight with both hands, then press it overhead until your arms are straight and your biceps are next to your ears. This is the starting position.
    3. Without moving your upper arms, bend your elbows to lower the weight behind your head until your forearms are at least parallel with the floor. Pause, then straighten your arms to return to the starting position.

    Exercise Benefits

    The main benefit of the overhead triceps extension is the hypertrophy of the triceps that comes with the motion. Unlike other triceps exercises, the triceps extension activates all three heads of the triceps, which means that your entire triceps will become stronger through this exercise.

  • Skull crusher

    - Primary muscle : Triceps
    - Secondary muscles : None
    - Equipment: Dumbbells (or barbell), weight bench.

    Skull crusher Instructions

    1. Flex your elbows and lower the weight down toward the top of your head. Your upper arms should remain relatively perpendicular to your body. This keeps the tension on the triceps versus shifting it to the shoulders.
    2. Continue lowering the weight behind the head. The bottom of the dumbbell head should be about in line with the bench's top, or even a little higher if this feels unwieldy.
    3. Reverse the movement until the weight is above the chest in the original starting position. Keep from locking the elbow to maintain tension in your triceps muscle.
    4. Repeat.

    Exercise Benefits

    Skull crushers are one such exercise. During the movement, your triceps work in conjunction with your pectorals and anterior deltoids to push the weight up and to control it during the lowering phase. The skull crusher is a good exercise to activate the medial head of the triceps, as compared to standing triceps extensions or other exercises in which your upper arm is at your side (4Trusted Source). This can help add mass to your triceps, which is important for bodybuilders.

  • Cable Tricep Kickback

    - Primary muscle : Triceps
    - Secondary muscles : rear deltoids, mid-upper back
    - EEquipment: Cables

    Cable Tricep Kickback Instructions

    1. Set up for the cable tricep kickback by attaching a single grip handle to a low pulley cable machine and selecting the weight you want to use.
    2. Grab the handle using your left hand with an overhand grip and take a step back from the cable machine.
    3. Keeping your feet together and knees slightly bent, bend down until your body is around parallel to the floor.
    4. Use your free right hand to stabilize yourself.
    5. Bring the elbow up as far as possible and keep it tight by your side. This is the starting position for the exercise.
    6. Keeping your elbow in place and your body fixed, extend your arm out as far as possible.
    7. Squeeze the tricep, and then slowly lower the weight back to the starting position.
    8. Repeat for desired reps.
